# Chapter 67 - Escort Graves

Bren's mother and Talin's sister led forty-seven relatives into Chimney Court carrying two empty helms.

No shouting. No riot flags. Just discipline sharp enough to cut.

"Tell us exactly how they died," Bren's mother said.

I gave compensation decree instead.

Two thousand ashmarks per family.
Lifetime ration rights for first-degree kin.
School exemptions for dependent children.

The clerk read. The gallery murmured approval. The families did not move.

"We asked cause," Talin's sister said.

"Enemy ambush," I answered.

True and incomplete.

I sealed cast details under operational secrecy. If displacement mechanics spread, every assassin would bait escort clusters to force the same choice again.

Rumor filled the silence by nightfall.

Some said witch-fire took them.
Some said I traded them for distance.

The second version held more truth.

Yarrow asked if we should issue denials.

"We confirm service. We deny specifics," I told him.

Recruitment data arrived a week later: escort-family volunteers down hard across two circuits.

Compensation preserved order.
Silence seeded distrust.

I put Bren's tags and Talin's knife in the same box as the unknown silver ring and locked it before dawn.
