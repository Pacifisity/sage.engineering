# Chapter 34 - Salt Lantern Summit

Tamsin convened the summit under bell-law.

Break terms under those bells and the curse bites lifespan directly.

At the table: two warlock captains, five Guild bankers, one warden proxy, me, and Irix pretending he wasn't representing anyone while tracking everyone's hands.

The offer was a cross-territory charter.

Recognize boundaries. Protect rotating convoys. Arbitrate disputes before they become raids.

In return, merchants stop funding immediate destabilization in my lanes.

I signed under blood oath because the charter bought time I needed against Sereth.

I didn't sign because I trusted anyone there.

The first convoys moved clean. No ambushes, no tariff wars, no broken lane pacts. Within weeks I was less warlord and more route infrastructure.

Irix called it useful.

Halvek called it dangerous.

"Constraint is part of power," he said. "Now prove you can keep one."

I spent three nights reviewing the oath language for loopholes.

There weren't any.

I wrote the entry as a strategic gain and a personal limitation.

It felt like both.
