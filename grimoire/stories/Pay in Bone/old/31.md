# Chapter 31 - Debt Surgeon Oath

Nara stopped mid-surgery, took off her gloves, and told me she was done covering for collateral damage.

"I can treat wounds," she said. "I can't keep cleaning up choices you call necessary."

Then she did something I didn't expect.

She invoked surgeon's debt law and bound me under vow.

Children under fourteen would be protected class in my operations. No direct targeting. No accepted child collateral under command authority.

Break it, and she leaves. Not quietly. With records.

I wanted to argue doctrine and battlefield reality.

Instead, I accepted and sealed it in blood.

A week later the vow got tested in a warehouse assault. We found four children hidden in an upper room after breach began. Extraction meant pulling fighters off pressure lanes.

I ordered extraction.

Two soldiers died in the gap.

The children lived.

Nara documented the transfer herself and said nothing in command review. But she sent me the clinic intake sheet with one line under it:

Vow kept under load.

That night I understood what she had done.

She didn't ask me to be kind.

She gave me a boundary with consequences strong enough to survive war.

In the ledger I recorded the tactical cost and wrote a second line beneath it.

Constraint accepted. Humanity retained by force.
