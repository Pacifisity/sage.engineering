# Chapter 30 - House Audit

Uncle Drov arrived with warden escort, polished documents, and no wasted words.

He called it a standard audit.

It wasn't.

He had traced ashmark serial clusters through my territory and found spending patterns that didn't match House books. In plain terms, he had discovered my independent treasury.

"Either you're funding unauthorized operations," he said, "or you're building power outside House control."

"I'm funding survival," I said.

He reviewed garrisons, routes, and clinic outputs for a week. He visited Nara's cellar and understood exactly what unofficial funds were buying: wounded retention, civilian stabilization, loyalty under scarcity.

At the end, he gave me the real deal.

He would report my campaign as effective and financially sound.

He would not report the treasury.

In return, I remained useful and bounded. No independent declaration. No overt break from House authority.

"Useful power is tolerated," he said. "Threatening power is removed."

I accepted because the alternative was Sereth auditing me personally.

Three days after he left, wardens raided three reserve sites with suspiciously accurate timing.

They hit hard. We evacuated two. The third held five wounded we couldn't move in time.

I chose not to mount a rescue.

The numbers said preserve network mobility and lose the fixed site.

The numbers were right.

Five names went into the ledger.

Halvek read them over my shoulder and said, "This is the line. Not power. This."

I didn't answer.

The Black Weight behind my eyes felt heavier than usual, like it recognized the decision as a milestone.

At dawn I reopened route planning and kept going.

That was the part that stayed with me most.
