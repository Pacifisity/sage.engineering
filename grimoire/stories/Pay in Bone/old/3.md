# Chapter 3 - Family Emergency

The week before the eclipse, House Veyr locked its lower archives and doubled interior guard rotations.

Official reason: external sabotage risk.

Real reason: paperwork.

By then I knew enough to watch the right hands.

Mother signed emergency continuity forms at dawn and dusk. Uncle Drov cross-filed witness attestations. Throne clerks came through private service doors instead of the public hall. Sereth moved between ritual chambers and legal offices with no escort, no interruptions.

I confronted Mother outside the ledger room.

"Are you declaring Father dead?"

"No," she said. "I am declaring this House functional."

"Without me?"

She adjusted one glove finger before answering.

"With safeguards."

The same day, two guard captains I trusted were reassigned to district patrol and replaced by men from Matron command rolls. My training staff changed. My key access changed. Even my meal schedule changed, always by legal notice, never by force.

That's how houses remove people when they don't want public blood.

By evening I was escorting a supply audit through the east corridor when I heard Sereth arguing with someone inside the surgery annex.

"There has to be another way," she said.

A male voice answered, low and clipped. Throne legal cadence.

"Emergency ratification permits transfer in cases of strategic instability. You have the signatures."

I stepped away before the floor guard saw me listening.

I should have run that night.

Instead I kept trying to solve it from inside the rules.

At last meal, Sereth sat across from me and barely touched her food.

"Stay in your wing tonight," she said. "No corridor rounds."

"Order or warning?"

She didn't answer.

I slept in travel clothes with boots beside the bed and my father's compass in my coat pocket.

At second bell, the house lights dimmed under ash eclipse and the corridor wards shifted to night lock.

Someone opened my door with a key only senior command should have held.
