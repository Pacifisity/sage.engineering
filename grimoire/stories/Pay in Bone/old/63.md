# Chapter 63 - Grave-Circle Trial

We cast at the west graves under rain and no lanterns.

Halvek placed the bronze forks. Lio marked ash lines and sequence checks. Morn held perimeter. Yarrow stood inside the outer ring with slate and timing marks.

I spoke the names in order.

First: silence.
Second: ground hum.
Third: distant signal braziers flutter.
By the sixth, vow scars along my ribs burned like wire.

Seventh: Ilyra Veyr-Caith.

The city stuttered.

Warden signal chains misfired. Tower orders crossed. Sereth's command quarter sent contradictory runners in open view. For seven minutes, her stolen Origin lost rhythm.

For seven minutes, she was vulnerable.

Then backlash took payment.

Not pain first. Loss.

A memory lifted clean: dusk courtyard, laughter, silver ring flash. Face blurred, name gone, voice fading last. I reached under my collar and found the plain ring still there, suddenly heavy with grief I could not place.

Halvek cut the rite before collapse. Yarrow and Morn dragged me out of the mud.

By dawn my shoulder barely turned and white had spread further through my hair.

Nara held up the ring during exam.

"Whose is this?"

"I don't know," I said, and meant it.

She gave one instruction only.

"Write down what you lost before you lose that too."

I logged the result:

Seven-name destabilization succeeded for seven minutes.
Major memory payment extracted.
Object retained: silver ring, provenance erased.

It was a turning point.

Not toward peace. Toward a sharper version of the same war.
