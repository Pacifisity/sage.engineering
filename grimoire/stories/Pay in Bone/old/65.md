# Chapter 65 - Ashland Tribute

Tribute circuit turned village squares into courts without benches.

I offered three payment channels: promissory harvest debt, direct cinder transfer, or bonded fighters for seasonal defense.

Publicly, elders thanked us for protection.
Privately, they called it polished extortion.

Both were accurate.

Dunmere paid in paper and reluctant spears.
Flint Hollow paid in cinders and hid draft-age sons.
Old Mire begged escort after refusing fighter quotas; I granted it at double interest.

"Punishment?" Yarrow asked.

"Instruction," I said.

By the fourth village, resistance was visible before speech: shutters closing on children, men standing too straight, priests blessing ledgers longer than graves.

Morn urged force normalization.
Irix argued for controlled resentment over open rebellion.

I followed Irix until the third night, when mirror prompts and bad weather wore my patience thin. We seized collateral lands from a council that missed tribute by two days and stamped transfer papers in mud while their elders knelt.

No blood spilled.
The message traveled anyway.

Next circuit compliance jumped.
So did hate.

I logged the month in two columns:

Resources improved.
Resentment embedded.

Both marked green.
