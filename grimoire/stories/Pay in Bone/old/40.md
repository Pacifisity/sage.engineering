# Chapter 40 - Archive of Teeth

The ossuary was older than city maps and colder than any crypt should be.

Its custodian traded in memory. For relics and coin, she brought us seven clay jars marked with Veyr seals. Inside each were milk teeth etched with kin-contract script: names carved into enamel, inheritance written in bite marks.

We opened the first jars and confirmed key lines quickly, including Vel Caith Veyr.

Then we hit the break.

One socket was empty. The tooth carrying a critical name had been removed long ago.

When I touched that jar, a ward snapped shut around my mind.

I saw the Veyr dead rising in burial ranks while Sereth stood among them, naming blood faster and cleaner than I could. In the vision, every name answered her.

I woke on stone with blood in my mouth and Lio gripping my shoulders.

"Preservation curse," he said. "It weaponizes fear around missing records."

The problem was that fear matched probability. Sereth had kept closer ties to family structures than I had. She might already hold what we were missing.

We spent two more days testing traces in the archive. Nothing.

Final count: six confirmed names, one void.

"Enough for a weakening rite," Lio said. "Not enough for a full collapse."

I accepted it.

Since the ward, memory had become harder to trust. Old gaps from bell-casting now filled with false fragments. Sometimes I pictured a relative and saw Sereth's face instead. Halvek called it cognitive fracture and gave the only cure I would not take: stop using the bell, stop touching cursed records.

I chose progress over stability.

By week's end we had infiltration routes, six names, and a ritual that might tilt the war without ending it.

I recorded it as operational advancement.

Privately, I wrote the part I could not say aloud: I am still deciding this with clear eyes, and that may be the worst sign of all.
