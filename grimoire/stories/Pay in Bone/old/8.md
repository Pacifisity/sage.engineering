# Chapter 8 - Mercy Is For Daylight

He cut my purse with a clean, practiced move, then looked annoyed when he found only bandage cloth and two cinders.

I caught his wrist before he could run.

Thin arm. Old bruises. Hunger rash at the neck. Twelve, maybe.

"Name," I said.

He tried to bite me.

"Name."

"Yarrow."

Market traffic kept moving like none of this existed.

I should have let him go.

Instead I bought two stale rye rolls and handed him one.

"Why?" he asked.

"Because you got close," I said.

I needed eyes wardens ignored, and he needed someone to pay him before someone else owned him.

By afternoon he had mapped routes I'd missed: three side alleys, a smuggler stair, and a drainage run under Warden Yard Eleven. Fast, sharp, and careful with details.

"Coin or protection?" he asked.

"Both if the work is clean."

He gave a short laugh. "Nothing is clean."

First courier run was at dusk. No names on the note. No repeated route.

He came back before full dark with Nara's reply hidden in his boot and someone else's blood on his sleeve.

"Patrol stopped me," he said. "I lied. One hit me. I ran."

His jaw was already swelling.

"If they corner you, drop the run," I said.

He shook his head. "Then the lane dies."

No drama in his voice. Just street math.

I took him to Nara.

She taped his ribs, cleaned the cut on his lip, then pulled me aside while he dozed against a crate.

"You are using him," she said.

"So is the city," I said. "At least with me he gets fed and paid."

"That's still not safety."

Outside, ward horns signaled a bounty sweep. New posters named me and listed known associates as accessories.

Nara folded one and put it in my hand.

"If he stays with you, he's marked."

I looked at Yarrow sleeping with one hand inside his coat like he expected theft even in his sleep.

"If he leaves," I said, "he doesn't make winter."

Nara didn't argue. She didn't agree either.

Over the next days he became essential: route checks, gate counts, courier drops, patrol pattern memory. He also saw the parts of me I didn't like seeing: threats, pressure, hard choices made fast.

On the fourth night he asked, "Are you trying to fix this place, or just survive it?"

I said, "Today, survive."

He nodded like that answer matched what he'd already guessed.

In my second ledger I wrote:

Entry two: Yarrow in network.

Then I added one more line.

Keep him alive.
