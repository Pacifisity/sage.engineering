# Chapter 28 - Wellfire

By first bell we had the poisoners in custody.

Nara came to command before market hour, still in blood-stiff gloves.

"Don't do this publicly," she said. "You're about to teach the district that justice and spectacle are the same thing."

"They poisoned children," I said.

"Then try them. Don't perform them."

I held the executions anyway.

The square filled. The ropes did what ropes do. The crowd watched in silence thick enough to feel.

I got deterrence.

I also got imitation.

Three days later, Yarrow intercepted a spy at his checkpoint and killed him without reporting up-chain first. Clean, efficient, no witnesses except his own unit.

When I confronted him, he gave me my own logic back.

"Execution deters," he said. "You taught that."

He wasn't defiant. He was precise.

That frightened me more.

I reassigned him to Irix and Halvek for detention review authority and told myself it was correction, not panic.

That night Halvek found me with the execution ledger open.

"You can still stop this line," he said.

"Can I?"

"Only if you decide winning has a boundary."

Nara's warning sat in my head with the names on the page.

I wrote a single note under the casualty table.

Deterrence achieved. Moral drift accelerated.
