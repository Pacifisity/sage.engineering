# Chapter 75 - Siege of Nine Chimneys

They ringed Nine Chimneys in three layers: sanctified infantry, hill knights, and hymn guns.

Relic trebuchets opened first. Choir shells followed, scrambling ward timing and command calls. By day two we were losing interior floors.

Morn wanted breakout with heavy losses.
Irix wanted artillery decapitation.
Halvek offered full pain-lattice integration.

"Link all nine chimneys through your nervous system," he said. "You become conductor. Cost may be permanent."

I agreed at dusk on day three.

Needles in spine points. Bronze forks at tower roots. Blood script on both forearms. Once linked, every enemy impact landed inside my body as electrical fire.

We held three more days and broke the siege lanes with synchronized pressure surges that turned every approach lethal.

Nine Chimneys survived.

When I shut the lattice down, my right arm did not move.

No pain. No response. Dead weight from shoulder down.

Nara tested reflexes, then said, "Possibly permanent."

I signed casualty rolls left-handed and wrote one sentence beneath them:

City preserved by sacrificing limb to architecture.
