# Chapter 26 - Sigil Hunger

Lio Ferr arrived with three ledgers and ash on his sleeves.

He had spent two days in temple basements translating old Veyr records.

"Stolen Origin work doesn't stabilize on pride," he said. "It stabilizes on blood."

He showed me two centuries of family rite logs. Every case where someone carried an Origin fragment without clean inheritance required recurring kin donations. Quarterly in some generations. Monthly in others. Always escalating.

Sereth had my sigil.

Which meant she needed Veyr blood to keep it from eating her.

Lio's second ledger listed recent disappearances from distant branch lines: river traders, minor clerks, two teenage cousins with no registered House ties. Quiet removals, regular cadence.

Maintenance.

"How many still reachable?" I asked.

"Forty-three," he said. "Scattered and mostly unaware."

I started a hidden census.

Locate every living Veyr branch relative. Move them under protection before Sereth's collectors reached them. If she couldn't feed the stolen sigil, she'd weaken over time.

Halvek read the same list and called it what it was.

"You're turning relatives into strategic inventory," he said.

"She already did."

"Yes," he said. "Now you are too."

Over four weeks, Irix and our contacts moved twenty-six relatives into safe locations. The oldest negotiated relocation like a merchant contract. The youngest came because their parents were told the truth in one sentence:

Your blood is now a target.

The other seventeen stayed out of reach, hidden, skeptical, or too far behind bad roads.

Sereth's response came fast.

More raids. Higher bounties. Marrow hounds pushed farther into our perimeter lanes.

She had felt the shortage.

That night I stared at the census and felt the Black Weight shift behind my eyes, as if it approved the logic.

I wrote one line in the ledger before sleep.

Countermeasure established. Family secured in part. Cost ongoing.
