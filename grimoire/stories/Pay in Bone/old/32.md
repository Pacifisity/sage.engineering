# Chapter 32 - The Nine Chimneys

The Nine Chimneys started as abandoned industrial towers and ended as my command spine.

We rebuilt the block in phases: stabilize foundations, install relay crowns, wire conduit channels, then activate the beacon grid.

When all nine stacks lit in synchronized flame-sign, the signal was visible across the district line.

Power made visible.

Then came labor policy.

Mandatory quotas in exchange for ration access. Work details for maintenance, expansion, and night-shift beacon support. It kept systems running. It also turned hunger into compliance.

Nara called it dressed-up coercion.

Irix called it a machine that would eventually demand more than we could feed.

Both were right.

Within months we had a permanent garrison, standardized ration office, ledger clerks, and a command relay network. We also had a population that could not leave the system without starving inside a different one.

Halvek watched first-light activation from the upper gantry.

"You wanted permanence," he said. "This is what it costs."

By then, I already knew.

If I walked away, the structure failed.

If I stayed, it consumed every hour I had.

I logged the transition as success in governance terms.

And trap in human terms.
