# Chapter 12 - Defrocked Counsel

I found Brother Halvek asleep behind a collapsed chapel wall, using a wine jug as a pillow and a relic hammer as a blanket weight.

Temple hunters were sweeping defrocked clergy that week. Halvek stayed alive by being useful to people who never wrote his name down.

When I kicked his boot, he opened one eye.

"If this is confession, get in line," he said.

"I need to cast without killing myself," I said.

That got his attention.

He listened while I explained the extraction damage, the tongue-vow, and the unstable pain channels. He asked practical questions. Where the pain started. How long it lasted. Which side failed first.

"You're forcing power through bad wiring," he said. "You need vow structure."

"Teach me."

"Hide me from temple hunters, and I will."

I trusted the deal, not the man.

I moved him to an unused bell room above the tannery. Nara objected immediately.

"You are not bringing drunk oathcraft into my clinic," she said.

Halvek gave her a formal bow that did not fit his clothes.

"I only drink when no one pays me to work," he said.

Nara looked at me. "If this kills someone, that's on you."

"It already is," I said.

Training started that night.

Halvek's core rule was simple: a vow isn't words, it's consequences. Weak vow, weak focus. Strong vow, stable output and brutal backlash if broken.

"Pick a vow you'll be tempted to violate," he said. "Otherwise it's theater."

I cut my palm and wrote on a cracked altar slab.

I will not kneel to House Veyr.

The bond took immediately. My breathing steadied. Hand tremor dropped. Channel control tightened for the first time since extraction.

We tested it on a patrol shaking down triage carts. I used a short cast and dropped the lead officer without opening a full blood-fire flare.

Clean output. No instant collapse.

By evening, temple auditors tagged the cast signature as heretic binding and raised my bounty tier.

Halvek read the notice and almost smiled.

"Now they take you seriously," he said.

Back at the tannery, Yarrow asked what a vow was.

"A promise that bites back if you break it," I told him.

He nodded like he understood exactly what that meant.

After he left, Nara stood in the doorway with her arms folded.

"You told him the clean version," she said.

"He's twelve."

"He's listening to everything," she said. "If you're going to build him into your network, don't let him confuse pain with purpose."

I wanted to argue. I didn't.
