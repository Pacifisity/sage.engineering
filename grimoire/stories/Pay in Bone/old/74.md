# Chapter 74 - Infinite Pressure

Halvek spread three maps across the forge table: casualty heat, spontaneous ward ignition, and mana density.

Every red zone overlapped.

"There is no ceiling now," he said. "Mass death is widening ambient flow. Progression caps are dissolving."

"For me?"

"For everyone. For you fastest, because you built infrastructure to absorb it."

That should have sounded like victory.
It sounded like sentence.

If catastrophe fed power and power produced catastrophe, then war had become its own fuel chain.

The mirror voices were ecstatic.

Scale now.
Become larger than ending.

At dawn I signed escalation orders: outer fortification, tribute increases where compliance held, fragment research tripled.

I wrote the logic in the margin so I could not pretend later:

If the system rewards catastrophe, refusing to scale is surrender.

Then I chose scaling anyway.
