# Chapter 70 - Nara Leaves

The break happened over a scouting order.

I approved a catacomb probe knowing first team losses were likely and useful for ward mapping.

Nara read the sheet, set it down, and asked one question.

"Will you go first?"

I did not answer.

Four scouts died by night bell. We gained three ward signatures and one partial route.

Nara did not come to the next council.

She sent notice instead:

Triage caravans continue.
Strategic attendance terminated.

No threat. No plea. Final grammar.

After that, clinic and command ran as parallel states sharing one city. We coordinated through manifests and couriers only.

At council I acted unchanged. Morn patched medical gaps with field sawbones and clerks. Halvek watched me avoid Nara's empty chair like it carried plague.

At night I unfolded an old map where Nara's hand still marked safe crossings in slanted ink. The notes had faded from rain and handling. I still slept with it under my blanket.

Yarrow found it once and quietly put it back when I woke.

In ledger I wrote two lines:

Medical coordination: functional.
Moral coordination: broken.

Then I moved to the next operation because moving had become the only thing I still knew how to do.
