# Chapter 60 - Vowbreak Fever

The vow breach looked minor until my body disagreed.

Convoy Oath 17-C required grain delivery to South Ward before night bell unless roads were impassable. I diverted around an ambush lane to save carts and broke the wording.

Backlash began before midnight.

Fever. Black-string blood cough. Sound distortion so severe that Yarrow's voice came through like bells underwater. My knees locked. I collapsed in a loft above dry stores.

Halvek arrived with leech bowls and reverse-meter prayer work.

"Vow debt compounds," he said while binding cords over old oath scars. "You are carrying too many live promises. One breach now detonates the stack."

He made me handwrite every active vow while shaking: supply, charter, quarry, protection, ritual, child-clause, transport. Six pages.

"New rule," he said. "No vow without exit plan. Discharge before expansion."

By dawn the seizure passed and left me older by another inch.

Nara checked pulse and stepped back before our hands touched.

"Maybe pain will teach what argument cannot," she said.

By afternoon I was back in command with hearing still warped and Yarrow repeating each order twice to confirm I had heard correctly.

I added a standing line to the operations book in ash ink:

NO VOW WITHOUT EXIT.

Then I resumed planning for the seventh name and the next breach point against Sereth, because recovery had become a pause, not a direction.
