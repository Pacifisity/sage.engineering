# Chapter 69 - Controlled Truth

I published confessions and called it transparency.

The packet was curated.

Three captains for ration theft.
One quartermaster for convoy-time sales.
Two magistrates for bond-child laundering before the White Hair Edict.

All true.
None of my worst truths included.

Crowds cheered in Chimney Court. Two bread riots dissolved without force. Recruitment rose in districts that read the release as courage.

Yarrow and Lio managed redactions. When I moved to strike a paragraph protecting a favored officer, Yarrow stopped his pen.

"Either this is rule or theater," he said.

"It is stability," I answered.

He marked it for partial release and wrote my compromise into the margins.

Nara sent one line by courier:

CURATED HONESTY IS STILL A LIE.

She stopped attending public ceremonies after that.

Operationally, the release worked. Mid-level corruption tightened. Officers self-reported minor offenses for amnesty before exposure cycles hit.

Morally, nothing changed: truth remained a tool, not a principle.

That night I listed three acts I had withheld.

Escort-anchor deaths.
Lime room execution.
Family funeral assassinations.

I folded the page, sealed it, and put it back in the dark.
