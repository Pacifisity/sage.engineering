# Chapter 66 - Karel's Scar Hunt

Karel found me on Saint Marrow Ridge where prayer wheels rusted in frozen wind.

We were traveling light: me, two escorts, one mule, no banners. It should have been invisible movement.

First sign was a nail-slice across Bren's cheek from nowhere.
Second was my mule dropping with a bolt through the eye.
Third was Karel's voice behind the wheel line: "You are easiest to track when you pretend to travel humble."

We ran for an ossuary wall and found him already ahead of us.

I cast emergency displacement.

The ward needed two blood-close anchors.
The escorts understood a half-second before it closed.

"Do it," Bren said.

I did.

Karel fired as the gate snapped. Bren took a throat shot. Talin caught backlash shrapnel and folded into the mud screaming. I displaced thirty yards into shadow and survived.

Karel did not pursue. He watched from the ridge and called down, "Distance bought. Dignity spent."

He was right.

I came back with Bren's tags and Talin's knife wrapped in map cloth.

At council I reported enemy contact and survival.
I did not report geometry.

In private ledger I wrote the true line:

Escaped by spending loyal men as coordinates.
