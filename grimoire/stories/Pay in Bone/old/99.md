# Chapter 99 - Broken Vow Price

The backlash took three days to arrive and one hour to break me.

First came tremor in the left hand. Then light sensitivity. Then full seizure during a command briefing, face down on map ink while colonels pretended not to look frightened.

Nara bled me twice and burned out clot lines with silver needles. Her verdict was clinical and brutal: the vow-break at Ashbridge had collapsed the pain channel I had used as stabilizer since the early campaigns. The channel had not just weakened. It had inverted. Every time I reached for that casting path now, my own nervous system tried to shut me down.

Victory, delayed defeat.

I adapted because adaptation is what predators and bureaucracies do.

Halvek built me a workaround matrix using distributed minor vows instead of one major lock. More flexible. Less reliable. Higher cumulative cost. I could still cast, but the old certainty was gone. Under pressure, my timing slipped just enough to kill the people nearest the channel if I misjudged range by even a handspan.

I ordered wider safety circles and shorter ritual teams. Everyone heard what I did not say: I had become another hazard on my own line.

The mirror delay worsened at night. In polished brass I would see myself blink after the room had already moved on. In still water I sometimes watched my reflected mouth form words I did not remember speaking. I stopped entering rooms with uncovered glass.

Yarrow requested authority to run two fronts without me physically present. I granted it. The request was practical. The timing was merciful.

In private ledger I wrote:

ASHBRIDGE RESULT: rival terminated.
CHANNEL TEN: nonrecoverable.
COMMAND CONFIDENCE: externally stable, internally degrading.
