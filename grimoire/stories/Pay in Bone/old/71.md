# Chapter 71 - House Veyr Civil Night

Sereth's legitimacy was thinning, so I cut it faster.

House Veyr had split into loyalists, opportunists, and frightened neutrals pretending to be both. I funded two branches through deniable channels and fed each forged orders proving the other had signed assassination contracts.

The first killing happened before midnight on East Veyr Row.

By second bell, manor districts were on fire. Guards torched archive rooms before rivals could seize debt ledgers. Servants and children were trapped in courtyards built for prestige, not evacuation.

I watched from a roof with Irix.

"You funded both sides," he said.

"Yes."

"Then this is yours."

He was right.

Nara's caravans crossed active kill lanes anyway. We coordinated corridor clearances by signal flag and did not exchange a single personal word.

By dawn, Sereth's command web had visibly thinned. Messengers ran double routes. Council meetings were canceled because no branch could guarantee safe attendance.

Operationally: tempo gained.
Humanly: arson by design.

I issued a public statement condemning "intra-House extremism" and offered mediation through Chimney Court.

In private I wrote the truthful line:

I fed both fires and called the smoke strategy.
