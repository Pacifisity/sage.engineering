# Chapter 6 - Count Your Ash Twice

Gloam Market opened before sunrise and started cheating people before first light.

By the time I crossed the district line, every lane was loud: ration flour, fake seals, stolen relic metal, lamp oil sold as medicine. Nothing had one price. Nothing stayed honest for long.

I was bleeding through a dead guard's coat and leaving drops when I walked.

A tea seller saw the blood and looked away. A clerk with a scarred chin signaled down the lane. I turned before he could follow and cut through a tannery gate where the steam hid scent.

Nara Quill's clinic sat in a backroom behind hide racks and sealed crates.

Her assistant tried to throw me out until he saw the extraction scar on my back.

"Get Nara," he said.

She arrived already gloved and put water on to boil without asking for my name.

"Who did this?"

"Family," I said.

She didn't waste time on sympathy. She cleaned the wound, reopened bad stitches, and threaded temporary sigil lines to keep the damaged channels from tearing wider.

It hurt enough that I nearly blacked out twice.

Halfway through, she asked the question that mattered.

"Who has your Origin now?"

"Sereth Veyr."

Nara's jaw tightened. "Then this city is in trouble."

When she finished, she pushed a fee slate at me.

Six ashmarks. One vial clotting agent. Two favors.

I put ten cinders on the table and the dead guard's knife beside them.

She took the cinders, pushed the knife back, and crossed out one favor.

"You still owe me one major favor and supplies," she said. "Three medicine crates were seized at Warden Yard Seventeen. Bring them back and I keep treating you. Fail, and find another surgeon before your spine gives out."

I nodded. We both understood this was a contract, not trust.

Outside, market bells hit third trade hour and warden sweeps started.

At brazier square, Sereth's face appeared in public flame script.

"Cael Veyr is a fugitive and murderer. Harboring him is treason. Reward for live capture."

Five hundred ashmarks lit up under her name.

The crowd went still.

A fish seller angled a stall mirror so I could see two men behind me in civilian coats and warden boots. I tipped the brazier stand, vanished into dye smoke, and ran until the shouting got distant.

Under an aqueduct, I checked Nara's stitches and the warrant list from the guard I killed. My name sat near the top.

No safehouse. No faction. One debt already due.

I burned the warrant from the corner and watched my own name curl black first.

By dusk I had one workable plan.

Steal Nara's crates from Warden Yard Seventeen.

Buy time with medicine.

Keep moving.
