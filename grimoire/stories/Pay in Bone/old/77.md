# Chapter 77 - Return of Nara

Casualties climbed past what field medicine could absorb.

Nara came back to council with terms, not sentiment.

Neutral clinic corridors.
No arrests or conscription inside marked lanes.
Violation names published on first breach.
Caravan withdrawal on second.

Morn objected on security grounds.
Irix objected on flexibility.
I agreed because I could replace officers, not her network.

By night, chalk sigils marked safe passage corridors through districts that had not shared rules in months. Rival wounded crossed under white cloth while armed patrols stood down and watched.

It looked weak.
It worked.

Survival numbers improved immediately.

Nara attended compliance audits and supply reviews only. No strategic planning, no personal reconciliation.

When she handed me first-week reports, our fingers touched. She withdrew first.

"Do not confuse cooperation with repair," she said.

I logged the outcome cleanly:

Lives saved by treaty.
Trust not restored.
