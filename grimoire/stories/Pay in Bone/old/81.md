# Chapter 81 - Fragment Rite One

I performed the first fragment rite beneath Nine Chimneys in the old turbine vault.

Halvek laid Second Origin geometry with seven copper pins. Lio logged every phase. Yarrow stayed outside the threshold so one person with full memory might remain if I failed.

Dual fuel was mandatory.

Fuel one: lifespan.
Fuel two: memory.

I paid six years and one anchored childhood season.

The split entered like ice through bone.

For a moment I held two channels at once: original current and fragment current, braided but different. Range expanded immediately. I could maintain defense lattice while projecting strike nets across district lines without relay collapse.

Then payment landed.

Rain lost smell.
A winter memory vanished clean: forge light, cracked blue cup, my father's laughter near dawn. I knew the shape. Content gone.

I burned the ledger edge after writing the losses so future me would trust the entry.

Morn called the rite breakthrough.
Nara called it self-amputation with theology.

Both were correct.

By week's end, fragment operations buckled enemy lines in two sectors and triggered panic in three civilian districts where power signatures appeared without warning.

That night my reflection lagged by half a breath and smiled first.

I recorded the summary and did not soften it:

Fragment acquired.
Erosion accelerated.
