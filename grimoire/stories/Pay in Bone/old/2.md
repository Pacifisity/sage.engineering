# Chapter 2 - Interim Authority

Interim authority is supposed to preserve a House until real leadership returns.

In practice, it lets people rehearse replacement.

Mother kept every decision legal. That was her strength. Every transfer stamped. Every seizure signed. Every emergency policy read aloud with witnesses present. If anyone challenged her, she produced three papers and a court citation before they finished speaking.

Sereth handled optics.

She visited district shrines, led healing rites in public squares, and spoke with the calm of someone already carrying inherited power. Crowds liked her because she looked steady. Clerks liked her because she never improvised in front of them.

I handled what they allowed me to handle.

Route inspections. Minor ward checks. House guard drills at hours no one important attended.

When I asked for access to the Origin chamber, I was told my focus discipline still needed review. When I asked again, Uncle Drov called it procedural delay and advised patience.

Patience is easier to preach when you already have authority.

One rain-heavy afternoon, a Throne assessor requested a demonstration of heir readiness before renewal of our emergency autonomy permits. I expected a combat test or strategic hearing.

Instead they asked for a controlled channel cast in the family chamber.

I passed the cast. Then a ward anchor failed in the wall seam and blew out half the chalk ring.

No one was hurt, but the assessor wrote for ten straight minutes.

"Unstable under stress," he said, not unkindly. "Recommend supervision increase."

Sereth stepped in before I could answer.

"He has been under unusual pressure since our father's disappearance," she said. "We'll tighten controls."

At dinner that night she sat beside me for the first time in months.

"They're watching for weakness," she said quietly. "Don't give them one."

"Then stop feeding them mine," I said.

She looked straight ahead.

"I'm trying to keep this House alive."

That was the first time I noticed we were using the same words and talking about different things.

Later, Yara from kitchen service slipped me a folded note while clearing plates.

Two lines only:

Throne clerks filed your cast under internal hazard review.
Matron requested expedited emergency options.

I burned the note in my lamp and told myself it was precaution, not a verdict.

I still slept with my father's compass under my pillow.
