# Chapter 7 - First Weight

You don't notice memory loss when it starts.

You notice it when you reach for something familiar and find nothing.

I watched Warden Yard Seventeen all morning from a roof across the canal.

Two patrol shifts. One clerk cycle. One blind corner where lime carts entered. Nara's three medicine crates sat under red tarp near the rear gate.

I couldn't brute-force this. No core, bad stitches, low blood.

At dusk I moved through mirror alley behind the yard and cast a minor concealment veil.

With a core, that veil would cost a cut and focus phrase.

Without one, it cost memory.

I sliced my palm, drew a spiral on dirty glass, and whispered the anchor line.

The veil took.

Cold rolled through my head. I crossed the service gate while two wardens argued about ration fraud. I cut the tarp, grabbed the crates, and almost got clear before one guard looked straight at the space I was standing in.

I pushed the veil harder.

Pain hit late, sharp and wrong, like cloth tearing inside my skull.

I dragged the crates through a lime chute into tannery lane. The alarm horn blew behind me. My shoulder reopened before I reached Nara's back door.

Her assistant hauled the crates in. I hit the floor.

Nara checked the seals, then looked down at me.

"What did you spend?"

"Not much," I said.

Lie.

Hours later, on a cot behind the hide racks, I tried to remember my father's voice.

I had his face. I had the way his hands moved over a map.

I did not have his voice.

I sat up too fast and tore a stitch.

Nara came in, fixed the seam, and watched me longer than usual.

"If you're casting blind without a core," she said, "you need to track the losses. Not tomorrow. Now."

In the morning she handed me dressings and spoke more softly.

"This kind of loss isn't neat. It starts small, then spreads. Be honest with me if it gets worse."

I nodded and didn't promise.

That night I opened a second ledger beside the vengeance list.

Entry one: Father's voice, gone.
