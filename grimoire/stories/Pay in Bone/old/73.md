# Chapter 73 - Envoy Fallout

Three days after Carth vanished, decrees arrived from three regions naming me envoy-killer and lawful correction target.

Then roads filled with armies.

Levy columns, shrine guns, sanctified wagons. Outer scouts reported the same shape every hour: more movement, tighter lines, less room for error.

This was no longer family war.
This was continental attention.

Ambient mana thickened with the pressure. Bell glass hummed without touch. Ward circles brightened at midday as if fed from below.

Halvek called it saturation.

"Too many rites in one theater," he said. "Too much vow debt and blood law. Casting gets easier. Misfire gets catastrophic."

I converted Carth's stolen maps into defense lattice plans while Morn ran mobilization and Yarrow reorganized archive evacuation in case Nine Chimneys fell.

No one asked whether killing the envoy had been worth it.

Nara sent one courier line:

BURN WARD EXPANSION STARTED. SEND MORPHINE IF YOU STILL REMEMBER PEOPLE.

I sent morphine.

By first snow, tribute towns were hedging openly, paying half and holding half for whichever army reached them next.

In ledger I wrote what the room already knew:

The war now has momentum larger than command.
