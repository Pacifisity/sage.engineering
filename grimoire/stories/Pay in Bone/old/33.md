# Chapter 33 - Sereth's Counter Rite

Sereth sent marrow hounds at gray hour.

Four of them. Bone-wired, pain-bred, tuned to my lattice signature.

Irix set three false trails with blood-resonance decoys. Three hounds split. One didn't.

The oldest stayed on me.

We ran through two districts before I turned into Slag Chapel and built a kill funnel from the broken transept and side vault walls.

When the hound entered the chamber, I burned my own blood to overload its tracking bond.

It worked.

It also burned deep through my left arm.

By the time Irix reached me, I could still move the limb but not trust it. Nara later confirmed permanent loss under load was likely.

Halvek's answer was a counter-rite: disrupt and rebuild my lattice harmonics so marrow tracking would fail future lock-on attempts.

The rite succeeded.

So did the side effect.

I became harder for enemies to track and harder for my own people to anchor in field operations. Safer and more isolated in the same stroke.

That night I wrote one clean conclusion.

Sereth can still reach me.

The price of staying ahead is paid in pieces.
