# Chapter 42 - Bone Clock Retaliation

I chose blood over leverage.

If Sereth's stolen power was fed through Veyr family channels, then cutting those channels could weaken her faster than any siege. We identified three junior cousins tied to ritual traffic and struck their funeral convoy before it reached the second ward gate.

Irix ran the operation. Three teams, six exits, no speeches.

It worked.

By evening, three Veyrs were dead and every House branch that mattered had my name underlined.

The response was immediate: formal blood vendetta, mercenary contracts in hill country, convoy raids, garrison probes. Not one war, but twenty small ones that never quite ended.

Halvek met me over the dispatch table and did not lower his voice.

"This is not strategy anymore," he said. "This is rupture."

"They were feeding Sereth."

"Some were trying to leave that system. You killed possibilities along with targets."

Nara gave me a different version in the clinic, hands red from work.

"You can still step away," she said. "Renounce, disappear, break the center point."

"If I step away, they come for everyone tied to me. You. Yarrow. The dock crews."

She held my gaze for a long time. "That was true the moment you made us your shield."

By month's end we had stabilized into attrition: too violent for peace, too costly for decisive victory. Merchants bled coin on escorts. Villages learned to hide food caches. Patrol logs doubled. Funeral logs tripled.

Karel appeared once at twilight, watching my map wall.

"You built yourself a war that cannot be won clean," he said. "Impressive engineering."

I told him to leave.

He smiled like a craftsman seeing good work in a broken machine.

In my ledger I wrote: vendetta active, equilibrium hostile, timeline shortening.

Under it, for myself only: I have become a problem that reproduces when cut.
