# Chapter 72 - Throne Negotiator

The Cinder Throne sent Envoy Carth in silk mail and polished courtesy.

His offer sounded elegant and meant surrender: shared governance in outer districts if I disbanded private warlock units and submitted ritual operations to Throne licensing.

I hosted him for three nights.

He believed he was measuring me for annexation. I was harvesting him for routes.

At dinner he gave patrol timings, shrine resupply rhythms, levy-bell windows, and relic wagon cycles disguised as small talk. On the final night he raised a cup and said, "History favors order over ego."

"Then we are both at risk," I answered.

He departed under white truce pennants at dawn.

I sent Irix after him at noon.

"Kill the envoy," I said. "Erase records."

Irix made me repeat it once, clean and audible, then left.

Carth was gone by dusk.

I gained maps and burned diplomacy in the same stroke.

Council called it decisive.

My ledger called it exact:

Intelligence harvest high.
Negotiation future destroyed.
