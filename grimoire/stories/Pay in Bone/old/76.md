# Chapter 76 - Nerve Burn

Four days after the siege, I tested the arm with a knife.

Wrist cut. No pain.
Elbow cut. No pain.

Only blood proving tissue still belonged to me.

The mirror called it optimization.

I wrapped the cuts and went to council.

If maiming was now structural, we needed structure for it. I expanded prosthetic workshops: hook rigs, brace joints, lever wrists, cargo harnesses. Quartermasters called it recovery doctrine.

Nara called it adaptation to mass harm.

"Mercy would have been fewer maimings," she said.

By week's end, one-armed lift crews and brace-fitted scouts were routine. The language shifted from wounded to converted.

I heard that shift and let it stand.

At night, phantom pain sparked in fingers I could no longer command. I logged the same line twice in two books:

Body becoming equipment.
