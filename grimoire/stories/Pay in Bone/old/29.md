# Chapter 29 - The Basilica Bell

Saint Vellum's bell tower leaned like it was tired of standing.

Halvek taught me bell-casting there because runners were too slow for the territory we now held.

The rule was simple.

Each ring carried command farther.

Each ring took memory in payment.

One ring cost about an hour. Two could cost a day. Three could tear out a week if I pushed range hard enough.

"You choose what to lose," Halvek said. "Not whether you lose."

First transmission saved a scout pair from a sweep column.

Price: one hour gone.

I checked my journal and found a blank where a meeting should have been.

Second transmission redirected a merchant convoy.

Price: one day.

I remembered the outcome, not the moments inside it.

Third transmission saved trapped couriers after a secondary ambush.

Price: one week.

I could still see Nara in my notes from that week. I could not remember the feeling of those conversations.

That was the part that hurt.

Not just loss.

Evidence of loss.

I started burning thin marks into my personal journal where memory had been removed, so future me would know there had been something there.

Halvek approved the method.

"You document the gap," he said. "That's how you stay coherent."

By month end we had used the bell several more times. Communication speed improved. Command cohesion improved.

My personal continuity degraded in clean, measurable cuts.

In the bell chamber mirror, the Black Weight watched me choose what to forget and seemed to learn from each decision.

I logged the month as operational success and personal attrition.

The words looked calm. My hand wasn't.
