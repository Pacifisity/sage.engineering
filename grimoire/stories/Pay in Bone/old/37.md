# Chapter 37 - Glassfields Ambush

Morn Hal chose the Glassfields because fire loves mirrors.

At noon her pyromancers lit the first line, and sunlight turned into blades. Towers shattered. Carts burned. Refugee crews who had come to salvage glass were caught in open ground before my militia could form ranks.

I made the call that won me the field and ruined it.

Instead of pulling civilians out, I sent fighters to hold the south cluster and deny Morn Hal the ridge approach. Tactically sound. Morally rotten.

In the third hour she changed the board. Her casters dropped the remaining towers in a chained collapse, and the entire basin became a storm of glass.

We took cover under beams and wagon frames.

The refugees did not know how.

When the air cleared, the dead were everywhere. Cuts too many to bind. Lungs full of glitter dust. Eyes ruined by flying shards. Morn Hal used the chaos to withdraw, satisfied with denial if she could not take possession.

The battle report called it a defended position.

Nara called it what it was.

"You turned a district into a wound," she told me, setting a jar of blood-flecked sputum on her table. "This is from people who never touched your lines."

By the second week, wind picked up the powdered glass and carried it into homes and market lanes. Coughing turned to bleeding. Masks became ration items. Children learned weather signs before they learned sums.

I funded clinics, filter cloth, transport, and grave crews.

None of it repaired soil, lungs, or trust.

Halvek read the casualty totals beside me that night. "You are past accident," he said quietly. "You made a choice and now you are managing its consequences like inventory."

He was right.

I still signed the next operations sheet before dawn.
