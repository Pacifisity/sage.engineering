# Chapter 62 - The Seventh Name

The seventh name waited below Saint Vellum in a crypt older than the church above it.

Lio carried the bone key. Halvek carried the wrapped bronze fork. I carried six names written on my forearm in ash ink where sweat would not blur them.

The reliquary opened with a wax crack and a smell like old leather.

Inside lay a skin strip marked in needle script:

Ilyra Veyr-Caith.

I spoke it once.

Stone answered.

Not light. Pressure. Voices in the joints of the crypt, ancestral tones shaped like judgment. One blamed Sereth. One blamed me. One said both.

Halvek watched my hand shake.

"This rite will not leave you clean," he said.

"Nothing has for years," I answered.

We left in rain, and I repeated the name every hundred steps so bell gaps could not steal it before cast time.

At camp, I wrote all seven names from memory, burned the page, and wrote them again. All seven returned.

For the first time in months, the objective was complete enough to execute and terrible enough to fear.
