# Chapter 102 - Sereth's Signal

The beacon rose over the ruin plains on a blood moon, visible from every tower in my command radius.

It was not fire. It was voice.

Sereth's cadence traveled through the light, each phrase splitting and recombining across ward lines like a choir of knives.

Inheritance rite, she called it.

Bring your true command self to the basin beneath Old Crown Spine. Bring no armies into the core ring. Bring one heir if you still pretend to have one. Refuse, and the civic lattice enters rain phase at first thaw.

I mobilized armies anyway. Three full columns moved along old king roads to secure exits, cut reinforcement routes, and keep the Throne from exploiting the convergence. But for the core site I selected a strike cadre of twelve: breach specialist, two ward breakers, four close blades, one scribe, one tunnel guide, Yarrow, and me.

Nara refused to come.

"I will not stand in another ritual chamber and call it medicine," she said. "My corridors stay open while you decide what kind of ruin you are." She still sent antitoxin kits and blood-stop salts with the cadre. Refusal without abandonment. That was her grammar now.

Yarrow insisted on joining. I told him this was not a proving run. He said, "Then stop using me as one everywhere else." I signed him in.

Before departure he gave me the brass key Irix had left and told me he had opened the vault two nights earlier. Inside was a single page in Irix's hand:

When she offers you the throne, read the plumbing.

In the margin: all engines lie in their control room and tell the truth in their intake pipes.

We entered the ruin plains at nightfall under no-banner discipline. Behind us, armies waited like clenched teeth. Ahead, the basin glowed red through ground mist, and every step felt like walking toward a verdict already written.
