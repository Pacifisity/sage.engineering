# Chapter 97 - Yarrow Breaks Rank

The execution order covered thirty-one prisoners captured at Salt Lantern after a sabotage raid on our ration rails.

Most were confirmed couriers. Five were armed lookouts. Three were barely old enough to shave. In the current doctrine, all thirty-one were expendable warnings.

I signed the order before dawn.

By dusk the cells were empty.

Yarrow had moved them through old smugglers' tunnels under the lime kilns, using scout channels I had taught him to build and hide. He left me a map, a single spent flare, and a note in his tight script: You needed fear. I need people who still listen when I ask for surrender.

Tribunal demanded immediate punishment. Halvek wanted rank stripping and confined command. Quartermasters wanted public censure to preserve chain discipline. Nara said nothing in council, but her eyes stayed on me the way surgeons watch a blade tremble.

I chose theater over truth.

In public I reframed Yarrow's action as controlled relocation of intelligence assets to shadow custody. Commendation for initiative. Expanded authority over scout amnesty channels. The room accepted it because no one wanted to watch our strongest young commander broken in front of troops already starving on too many fronts.

In private ledger, I entered a different note.

YARROW: loyalty retained. independent moral axis active. succession risk index elevated.

When I confronted him alone, he did not apologize. "You told me once we were building a world where surrender meant survival," he said. "If that's no longer true, tell me now so I can stop lying for you." He spoke like a man twice his age and a child still asking permission.

I told him to keep his channels open.

That was not forgiveness. It was recognition that I could no longer command this war with men who had amputated every instinct I once called virtue.
