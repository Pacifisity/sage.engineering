# Chapter 79 - The Mother's Last Bargain

My mother sent terms under her own seal.

Vault coordinates in exchange for safe exile through neutral abbeys.

No apology language. Just logistics.

I accepted the coordinates and denied the exile.

We intercepted her convoy at Ashbridge with minimal blood: two guards disarmed, one wrist broken, no deaths.

She stepped down and looked at me as if this outcome had always been likely.

"You chose efficiency," she said.

I placed her under black archive status: no public trial, no execution, no active legal identity.

Vanishing by paperwork.

Before the vault door closed, she handed me one extra marker and said, "The second lock is not blood. It is regret."

Then she disappeared from every registry that mattered.

In official record I wrote: Matron Ys transferred under sealed authority.

In private record I wrote the truer line:

No corpse. No absolution. Same weight.
