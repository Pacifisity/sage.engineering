# Chapter 78 - The Child Commander

Yarrow commanded roof scouts by then.

Not courier work. Not symbolic rank. Command.

Teen crews moved over canal roofs and collapsed chapels where adult teams made too much noise. Yarrow gave orders from gutters with no tremor in his voice.

"Two north to bell lane. One cut runner if he reaches shrine wall. No chase past lantern line."

That night his report listed three confirmed kills and one denied shot because target carried a child. He marked the denial as discipline success.

At debrief I asked, "Do you sleep?"

"Enough," he said.

"Do you dream?"

"Only routes."

In public, command used him as propaganda: proof the movement raised talent from ruin.

In private, I recorded the cost with no euphemism:

Loyalty intact.
Childhood unrecoverable.

Nara confronted me after youth casualty rolls posted.

"You made children into doctrine," she said.

She was right. I still signed the next deployment sheet.
