# Chapter 27 - Grain Vault Raid

The break-in worked.

The aftermath didn't.

We hit the Guild grain vault on Merchant's Rise before dawn. Irix and I took point. Four guards dropped quietly. Lock failed on the third pressure cycle. We loaded forty-three sacks and moved them into lower-ward distribution points.

Nara had planned for controlled lines.

Instead, word spread faster than carts moved.

Hundreds hit the first lane. Then more. Queue discipline collapsed into crush. Two stabbings, one trampling death, and a wave of panic that turned food into a weapon before breakfast.

"Stop distribution," Nara ordered when she found me. "We're feeding a riot."

We shut it down, reset by district lists, and resumed in controlled batches by noon.

By sunset, the Guild answered.

Three well systems in the lower wards were poisoned with caustic runoff.

Nara's cellars flooded with patients. Split lips, internal burns, dehydration collapse. She looked at me over a table full of survivors and said, "This is retaliation for your raid, and we're the ones paying it."

For six days we ran clean-water convoys under armed escort.

Forty-nine dead. Nineteen critical. More with permanent injury.

We captured six paid poisoners and made the punishment public. It stabilized fear, not trust.

Irix reviewed the numbers at the end of the week.

"Food security improved," he said. "Territory support increased. Casualties severe but within historic range."

"And?" I asked.

"People don't think you saved them," he said. "They think your help is dangerous."

He was right.

In the ledger I recorded the operation as both win and warning.

Vault raid successful.

Civilian confidence unstable.

Infrastructure gap exposed.
