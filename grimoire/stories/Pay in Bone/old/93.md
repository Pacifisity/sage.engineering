# Chapter 93 - Housefall

We struck every remaining Veyr branch House in one night so no messenger could outrun the fire.

The operation map looked elegant: twelve targets, four assault waves, bell-timed breaches, bridge controls locked by quarter hour. In practice it was mud, screaming, jammed loaders, and ward teams vomiting from overchannel while trying to hold anti-gate fields against sanctum counter-ritual.

At First Bell, North Bastion fell after a powder magazine chain burst under the east keep.

At Second Bell, the Ledger House archives burned from basement upward, paper rising through shattered rafters like black snow.

At Third Bell, the old Veyr chapel rang continuously as if someone had tied the rope to a storm. We found no rope. No keeper. Only a blood-smeared altar and three novice scribes who had cut their own palms raw trying to keep a gate circle stable long enough for their betters to run.

By dawn every branch keep was either collapsed, surrendered, or reduced to a smoking shell with our seizure banners over the gate. House Veyr, as an institution, died that morning. Its treasuries, oath books, creditor chains, and hereditary warrants were in our custody by noon.

Sereth was not.

At South Vault Annex, her guard captain triggered a blood gate keyed to living marrow signatures. We reached the chamber in time to see the ring collapse inward, taking half the floor and six of my assault team with it. On the wall behind the gate she left one sentence in red chalk: YOU TOOK THE HOUSE. I TOOK THE WEATHER.

Nara read the line and closed her eyes for a long time before saying, "Then this is not an ending."

No, it wasn't. We had removed the old architecture of power while the new mechanism was already in motion underground. The city cheered the fall because cheering is what people do when one monster finally drops. I let them have it for a day. They deserved one unbroken night.

In command ledger, I logged the campaign outcome as HOUSE VEYR TERMINATED. STRATEGIC THREAT MIGRATED. Then I added a private note beneath it, one no archivist will ever catalog: Victory now arrives looking exactly like warning.
