# Chapter 35 - Currency War

The Guild flooded border markets with counterfeit ashmarks.

Good metal, good stamps, wrong trust.

Prices tripled in days. Merchants hoarded verified coin and pushed suspect coin down-line. Payrolls jammed. Militia confidence started to slip.

Torvin said we could spend weeks proving legitimacy.

We didn't have weeks.

So we minted cinders as a second currency, each one sealed through my direct authentication pattern. Slow, expensive, hard to forge.

Nara and Irix pushed adoption from below: clinic fees, ration lines, convoy pay, and district contracts all accepted cinders first.

The split economy stabilized faster than expected.

Ashmark markets stayed volatile.

Cinder markets held.

Halvek watched me spend half my nights authenticating batches and said, "You just built a central bank by accident."

He wasn't wrong.

The attack meant to collapse my payroll ended by tying local economic trust directly to my continued presence.

If I disappeared, cinders would fail.

If I stayed, I became responsible for monetary stability as well as war, governance, and survival.

In the ledger I marked the month as defensive success.

Then added the line that mattered.

Control expanded. Exit narrowed.
