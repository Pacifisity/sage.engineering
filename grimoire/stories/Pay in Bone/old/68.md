# Chapter 68 - Yarrow's Ledger Theft

I found Yarrow in the archive crawlspace with Ledger B under his coat.

He did not run.

"Put it down," I said.

"Not unless we change the system," he answered.

Lio arrived furious, breathless, already reaching for guards.

Yarrow faced both of us and spoke like a clerk presenting policy:

"Keep murder, treason, convoy sabotage. Burn leverage pages on debt shame and private sins. We are governing with permanent blackmail."

He had planned reform by burglary.

I should have punished him.

Instead I called emergency council and issued a restructure.

Ledgers remain active.
Custody moves to Yarrow under oath-chain supervision.
Access requires dual witness: Yarrow and Lio.
Nonviolent confessions older than one year move to sealed amnesty annex.

Lio objected until I made the alternative explicit: full trust collapse.

Yarrow took custody oath in quarry hall without shaking.

"I will guard this archive against enemy breach and command abuse," he said.

The room heard the second half.

Afterward he asked me quietly, "Did I do wrong?"

"You did necessary," I said.

"Not the same thing," he replied.

"No."

For the first time in years, power moved out of my hand and the structure got stronger.
