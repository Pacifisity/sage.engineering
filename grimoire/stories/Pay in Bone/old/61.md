# Chapter 61 - Basilica Revisit

Saint Vellum's tower still leaned where I had cracked it months earlier.

Pilgrims now knelt to the ruin itself. Faith had adapted to rubble.

I came for bell bronze.

Halvek needed sanctified alloy for the harmonic forks in the seven-name rite. The basilica frame was the last nearby source with enough purity.

When my crews hauled rigging into the nave, people cursed and prayed in the same breath.

"You broke it once," an old woman shouted. "Now you will strip it too?"

"Yes," I said. "We are out of cleaner options."

We cut segments from cracked yokes while roof scouts watched for sniper flash. Bronze rang dull under rain. Every shard held warped reflections; every reflection carried whispers I pretended not to hear.

Yarrow caught me answering one aloud.

"Who are you talking to?"

"Wind," I said.

He did not believe me.

By dusk we hauled seven loads and buried two riggers crushed by a frame slip. Halvek tested the metal and gave one short nod.

"It will hold."

Nara sent no message about the dead.

That silence landed harder than accusation.

In the logistics ledger I recorded weight, purity, and casualties.

In my private book I added the line I almost erased:

The city worships ruins now because I keep proving ruins are honest.
