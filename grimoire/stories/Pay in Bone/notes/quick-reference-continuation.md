- Debt archive burned in public.
- Retaliatory search/seizure powers expanded.
- Sereth escaped with two ledger pages.
- Nara left with Cael's child.
- Yarrow acts as autonomous field officer.

## Current Time/State
- Roughly three years from exile by end of Ch 15.
- Cael physically degraded, psychologically compartmentalized.
- Governance is now the primary battlefield.

- Bell-casting: memory severance (hour/day/week scale).
- Archive freeze cast: three months lifespan burned.
- Ongoing scar lattice attrition: pain, injury, reduced recovery.

## Location and Control Locks
- Nara relocates to a different city under a different House.
- The city formerly under House Veyr is under Yarrow's control after the attrition period.

## Succession Rule (Locked)
- Cael dies in his endgame confrontation; he does not return as a coherent self.
- Black Weight harvests Cael's cores (gathered Origins) and complete memory architecture at the moment of death.
- Black Weight \"rewards\" both harvested cores and memories to Kess as compensation for Kess accepting Black Weight's attachment.
- The transaction is power-for-hosting: Kess inherits actual magical capacity plus memory, in exchange for Black Weight gaining a new host body.
- This is not burden inheritance but active exchange; Kess enters as MC with resource advantages unusual for age.

## Perspective Rule (Locked)
- Use single-MC perspective only.
- Current MC: Cael.
- Next MC after transfer event: Kess (female).

## Timeline Locks
- Sereth's attrition war runs for 3 years.
- Kess is 3 years old when Cael performs his terminal sacrifice.
- As Kess grows, references to Nara's new-House city and Yarrow-held Veyr city should remain subtle and recurring.

## Near-Term Focus (Ch 16-20)
- Frontline civil-uprising warfare, not abstract policy-only conflict.
- Sereth runs attrition pressure until Cael finally defeats her.
- Matriarch Ys immediately harvests Sereth's Origin to become the primary apex threat.
- Drov is later betrayed and consumed by Matriarch Ys in a loyalty-destruction beat.
- Yarrow handles first-order system failures that predate the split and worsen after Nara's abrupt departure.

## Chapter Construction Checklist
- Show one concrete scarcity detail (price, stock, route, queue).
- Show one operational decision with nonlocal fallout.
- Show one relationship consequence that persists.
- Show one cost acknowledgment in or near magic use.
- End with a forward pressure, not a clean resolution.

## Red Flags
- Character reversion to pre-Ch 10 behavior.
- Major casts with no durable cost.
- Policies with no downstream victims.
- Emotional resets after major breaks (especially Cael/Nara).
