# Power System: Grim Cant (Current Canon Through Ch 15)

## Core Law
No cast is free. Payment is required in blood, pain, memory, lifespan, vows, or transferred burden.

## Origin Sigil Rule
- Origin Sigil grants stable casting and lineage legitimacy.
- Cael's Origin Sigil was stolen.
- Cael casts through scar architecture and explicit cost declaration.
- Stolen power remains unstable without ongoing resonance supply.

## Cost Methods
- Lifespan: direct age drain for high-load operations.
- Blood/Pain: immediate channel fuel and anchor.
- Memory: durable loss, especially in bell-casting.
- Vow: behavior-bound magic that punishes breakage.
- Transfer: burden displaced onto others through debt/pact structures.

## Bell-Casting Cost Math (Canonical)
- 1 ring ~= 1 hour memory loss.
- 2 rings ~= 1 day memory loss.
- 3 rings ~= 1 week memory loss.
- Cost scales nonlinearly with range and complexity.

## Scar Lattice Rule
- Enables no-core casting through pain channels.
- Grants reach but compounds chronic injury.
- Repeated high-load use increases failure and recovery lag.

## Black Weight Progression
- Starts as mirror/perception anomaly.
- Evolves into strategic whisper and affect flattening.
- By Ch 10-15 behaves like a co-pilot logic layer.
- It is useful, accurate, and corrosive.
- Terminal ability (endgame): harvests cores and memory architecture from host at death; can reward harvested power to new host in exchange for attachment continuation.

## Established Chapter 10-15 Cost Events
- Ch 10-11: repeated bell-casts with day/week memory severance.
- Ch 14: interior freeze operation paid with three months lifespan.
- Ch 15: siphon disruption chosen over clean kill; tactical win with civic injury.

## Anchors
Persistent effects require anchors:
- names
- relics
- scars
- structures
- vows

## Chapter 16+ Power Guardrails
- Never hand-wave recovery after major casting.
- Tie costs to behavior changes, not only physical symptoms.
- Keep civic-scale magic tied to civic-scale fallout.
- Keep Sereth's instability linked to supply constraints, not mood.
