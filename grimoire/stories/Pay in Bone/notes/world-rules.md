# World Rules and Tone

## Setting Tone
- Apocalypse-era city-states powered by ritual industry, famine, and war pacts.
- Atmosphere is bleak, predatory, and occult; mercy is expensive.

## Social Hierarchy
1. The Cinder Throne (imperial clergy-war office)
2. High Houses (bloodline power brokers)
3. Wardens (licensed enforcers and tax captains)
4. Guild Chains (merchant-militant unions)
5. Free Districts (unstable, taxed labor neighborhoods)
6. Debtbound (indentured bodies and ritual collateral)

## Currency
- Primary currency: ashmarks.
- 1 ashmark = 16 cinders (small coin).
- Bone promissories are legal debt slips tied to blood signatures.

## Local Idioms
- "Count your ash twice" (verify the price before agreeing).
- "Mercy is for daylight" (kindness does not survive power politics).
- "Pay in bone" (every bargain extracts a lasting cost).
- "A clean sigil is a liar" (power always stains someone).

## Power System: The Grim Cant
- No hard ceiling to growth; the world leaks abyssal pressure, so total available mana expands after large disasters.
- Power growth requires surviving increasingly severe costs and contradictions.

### Core Rules
1. Every cast needs a tithe: blood, memory, lifespan, pain, vow, or another life.
2. Lasting constructs require an anchor (name, relic, place, or scar).
3. Repeated casting creates Black Weight (psychic debt), causing hallucinations and moral flattening.
4. Stolen lineage power is unstable until fused through kin-blood rites.
5. Major rituals trigger social consequences (new taxes, sanctions, hunts, or retaliations).

### Main Progression Costs
- Blood cost: anemia, tremors, organ strain.
- Memory cost: identity erosion, missing faces, false recollections.
- Lifespan cost: visible aging and brittle recovery.
- Vow cost: magical backlash when promises are broken.
- Sacrifice cost: guilt fixation and escalating Black Weight.

## Continuity Law
- Every chapter must produce at least one persistent consequence that appears later as debt, ruin, altered alliances, or transformed locations.
