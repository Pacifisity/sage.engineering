# World Rules and Tone (Current Through Ch 15)

## Tone Baseline
- War-famine occult realism.
- Institutional predation over heroic clarity.
- Victories are transactional and partial.

## Political Structure
1. Cinder Throne: legal-theological war authority.
2. High Houses (Veyr): bloodline legitimacy and succession violence.
3. Wardens/shrine law: enforcement, seizure, inspection, transfer control.
4. Guild chains and smugglers: parallel logistics economies.
5. District governance blocs: survival administrations built under coercion.

## Economy Rules
- Ashmarks and cinders remain practical currency language.
- Control of roads, ledgers, clinics, and depots equals sovereignty.
- Policy is weaponized logistics.

## Governance Rules
- Administrative acts are never neutral under scarcity.
- Every decree benefits one population and injures another.
- Crowd promises create political liabilities even when not magical vows.

## Magic-in-World Rules
- Costs are mandatory and legible.
- Large rituals trigger market, legal, and medical aftershocks.
- Bloodline theft creates long-term instability at system level.

## Chapter 15 World State Snapshot
- Debt ledgers burned publicly; symbolic legitimacy spike.
- State retaliation expanded search/seizure and clinic pressure.
- Sereth escaped with partial archive intelligence.
- Nara's departure removed the strongest internal ethical brake on Cael.

## Chapter 16+ World Guardrails
- Show concrete effects: prices, shortages, raids, route closures, crowd behavior.
- Keep territorial war grounded in infrastructure, not abstract banners.
- Keep legal paperwork as a weapon layer in every campaign.
