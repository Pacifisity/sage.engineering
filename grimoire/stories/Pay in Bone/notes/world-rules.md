# World Rules and Tone

## Tone Baseline
- War-famine occult realism, not heroic fantasy.
- Institutions are predatory; every "solution" redistributes harm.
- Victory language should feel transactional, never clean.

## Political Structure (Active in Ch 1-12)
1. Cinder Throne: legal-theological war authority.
2. High Houses (especially Veyr): bloodline legitimacy and succession violence.
3. Wardens: enforcement + tax + convoy coercion.
4. Guild Chains: commerce, smuggling leverage, and market sabotage.
5. Free districts: governed by scarcity systems and armed logistics.
6. Debtbound populations: labor collateral and ritual supply base.

## Economy and Governance Rules
- Ashmarks remain primary coin; cinders function as fractional currency.
- Control over roads, food, and clinic access equals political sovereignty.
- "Humanitarian" policy usually doubles as command architecture.

## Magic System (Grim Cant) Rules
1. No cast is free: cost must be paid in blood, memory, lifespan, pain, vow consequence, or transferred burden.
2. Anchors are mandatory for persistence: names, relics, graves, scars, structures.
3. Black Weight accumulates with repeated high-load use: perception distortion, flattening empathy, compulsive optimization.
4. Stolen lineage power remains unstable without ongoing blood architecture.
5. Large ritual events produce civic aftershocks (pricing shocks, reprisals, policy hardening, insurgency).

## Oath Logic (Critical)
- Current Cael vow rule: he does not cast without naming the cost (explicitly or symbolically in-scene).
- Vow breaches must carry durable operational and bodily consequences.
- Avoid "free reset" scenes after major ritual strain.

## Character-Time Continuity Rules
- Yarrow age progression:
	- Early arc can state around twelve.
	- Mid/late arc should prefer relative age language unless a hard dated skip is established.
	- Do not reintroduce conflicting hard-number ages casually.
- Cael progression:
	- From insurgent operator to coercive administrator by mid arc.
	- Language should reflect command burden, not lone vigilante framing.

## Conflict Logic Guardrails
- House conflict is active territorial war; avoid passive bureaucratic framing unless it is explicitly weaponized cover.
- Face-to-face diplomacy between mortal enemies should be rare, high-risk, and tactically justified.
- If a faction applies pressure, show force vectors (roads, depots, clinics, relays), not abstract politics.

## Continuity Law
- Each chapter must leave at least one persistent scar in one or more domains:
	- Body
	- Relationships
	- Infrastructure
	- Legitimacy
	- Resource systems
