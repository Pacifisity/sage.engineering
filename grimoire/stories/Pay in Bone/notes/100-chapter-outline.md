## Arc Blocks 16-100

- Civil-uprising warfare dominates chapter ground action.
- Sereth prosecutes an attrition campaign to pin Cael in direct conflict.
- Cael defeats Sereth, but Matriarch Ys harvests Sereth's Origin at the point of victory.
- Drov is consumed by Matriarch Ys during power consolidation.

- Matriarch Ys operates with drastically reduced visible casting cost.
- Cael commits to a terminal sacrifice path to stop Matriarch Ys.
- Cael dies; Black Weight harvests both his cores (Origins) and his complete memory architecture.
- Black Weight "rewards" harvested power and memory to Kess in exchange for attaching to her; establishes next-MC inheritance through active transaction, not mere transfer.

### Arc C (36-45): Kinline Fragmentation

### Arc D (46-55): Regional Expansion Cost

- New allies demand policy concessions.
- Currency, ration, and clinic systems buckle and adapt.

### Arc E (56-65): Mirror State

### Arc F (66-75): Siege of Legitimacy

### Arc G (76-85): Bloodline Collapse Campaign
- Kinline infrastructure reaches breaking threshold.
- Multiple factions try to control succession narrative.
- Cael forced into irreversible lineage decisions.

### Arc H (86-95): Endgame Administration
- Surviving systems become increasingly brutal to sustain.
- Black Weight influence approaches full behavioral control.
- Characters choose what they are willing to preserve.

### Arc I (96-100): Final Cost Settlement
- Resolve inheritance of power, not just winners of battles.
- Land final consequences for Cael, Sereth, Yarrow, and Nara.
- End on cost accounting, not triumphant closure.
