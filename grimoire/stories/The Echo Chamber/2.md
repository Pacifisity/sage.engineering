# Chapter 2: Phase Two

Nate replied to Rook with the speed of panic.

> There is no Phase Two.

Rook responded three seconds later.

*Understood. Phase Two remains unnamed for operational security.*

Nate dropped the phone on his desk like it had burned him.

---

By Wednesday, Echo Chamber members were posting photos from outside utility buildings, telecom closets, traffic control boxes.

They called them "Listening Points."

Nate called them trespassing.

His words had stopped meaning what he thought they meant.

He tried being explicit.

> Stop going places. This is not a game. You can get arrested.

Replies:

*He's testing loyalty.*

*He has to deny it publicly. Learn to read subtext.*

*The Drift Code is obvious if you've done the work.*

There was no Drift Code.

There had never been any code.

One user posted a ten-page PDF explaining the hidden structure of Nate's timestamps. Another translated his typo from last week into a numerical cipher that allegedly mapped to city districts.

The PDF had footnotes.

People called it *The Wilkes Sequence*.

Nate sat in his kitchen and laughed until the laugh collapsed into something else.

He sent an audio message to his sister and deleted it before pressing send.

He drafted an apology post and deleted that too.

At 4:17 p.m., local news ran a segment titled: **ONLINE MOVEMENT SPARKS INFRASTRUCTURE PANIC**.

They used his profile picture.

At 4:32 p.m., his manager told him to take unpaid leave "until things cool down." His ID card stopped opening the front door.

At 5:05 p.m., someone taped a note to his apartment lobby intercom:

*WE HEAR YOU. 2:00 IS THE CRACK.*

---

He scheduled a livestream for 8:00 p.m. to end it.

He wrote a statement in plain language.

No metaphors. No questions. No room for interpretation.

*I am not a leader. There is no plan. There is no code. You are hurting people and yourselves. This stops now.*

At 7:58 p.m. he almost cancelled.

At 8:00 p.m. he went live.

Eighteen thousand viewers joined in under a minute.

The chat exploded.

*THE ARCHIVIST SPEAKS*

*PHASE TWO BRIEFING*

*listening points reporting in*

Nate read the statement once, then again, slower.

For a moment, the chat seemed to stagger.

Then Rook posted:

*If this is all fake, explain tonight's outage grid at exactly 20:00.*

As if on cue, the lights in Nate's apartment dimmed and came back wrong.

His microwave clock blinked 7:58.

His oven blinked 7:58.

His wristwatch skipped backward and held.

The chat became a flood.

*CONFIRMED*

*HE WARNED US*

*PHASE TWO ACTIVE*

Nate stared at the blinking clocks and felt something in his chest go thin and sharp.

He ended the stream without saying goodbye.

---

After midnight, there were videos of crowds in three cities standing in intersections at 1:58, waiting.

Police scanners leaked fragments.

"...group gathered around municipal timer..."

"...refusing to disperse..."

"...calling themselves listeners..."

At 2:00, someone smashed the glass over a downtown transit control panel.

At 2:01, two buses collided at low speed in the rain. No deaths. Nine injuries.

At 2:04, Rook posted:

*Sacrifice is the tax of truth.*

Nate read that sentence and threw up in his sink.

He scrubbed his face with cold water, looked at himself in the mirror, and did not recognize the man looking back.

His phone rang from an unknown number.

He answered because it was easier than thinking.

A calm voice said, "Mr. Wilkes? This is Agent Lian Morales with the Department of Infrastructure Integrity. We need to talk about your community before sunrise."

Nate leaned both hands on the counter and tried to breathe.

"I didn't mean for this," he said.

Agent Morales replied, "I believe you. That's why you're still on this side of the call."
