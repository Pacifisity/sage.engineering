# Chapter 7: The Demon King Sends a Letter

The letter arrived on a Tuesday, sealed in black wax stamped with a skull wearing a small decorative bow.

The courier was a nervous man in smoke-gray livery who refused to make eye contact and left at a brisk walk the moment the letter changed hands.

*To the Summoned Hero of Dawnreach,*

*Greetings from the Obsidian Citadel.*

*It has been approximately seven weeks since the prophecy was activated and your arrival confirmed through our standard intelligence channels. We had initially projected a confrontation within the first fortnight. That projection has since been revised three times.*

*I write not out of hostility but out of scheduling necessity. The Final Darkness has been calendared for the Spring Equinox. We have caterers.*

*Is that still viable from your end? I ask only because my generals are on rotation and I have promised the eastern battalion leave through late spring. If you plan to arrive earlier, a week's notice would be sufficient.*

*If the Equinox no longer suits, I am willing to discuss alternatives, though I should note that summer is quite busy.*

*Awaiting your response,*
*Mordavel, Demon King, Lord of the Obsidian Citadel, Bringer of the Unending Night, Etc.*

Ren read the letter four times.

"He has caterers," Ren said.

"Apparently," said Petra.

"For the end of the world."

"He calls it the Final Darkness."

"Same thing." Ren turned the letter over, as if the back might contain clarifying footnotes. "He sounds... organized."

"He is the ruler of an army of shadow and ash, yes."

"But also flexible about scheduling."

Elda took the letter and read it herself. Her expression moved carefully through several phases before settling on something like resigned acceptance of a universe that was clearly doing whatever it wanted.

"This is unprecedented," she said.

"Is it, though?" Ren reached for a pen. "He's been waiting. I keep not showing up. From his perspective, I've been very rude."

Elda grabbed Ren's wrist. "What are you doing?"

"Writing back. It's polite. He wrote first."

"You cannot **correspond** with the Demon King."

"He sent a courier. That implies a return address." Ren gently freed the wrist. "I'll be professional about it."

Elda looked at Petra. Petra was already opening a new page in her journal.

Ren's reply:

*Dear Demon King Mordavel,*

*Thank you for your letter and for your flexibility. The Spring Equinox remains on my radar. I am currently in a period of intense preparation and am targeting a final approach date somewhat before then.*

*Could you confirm whether the southwestern gate is the primary access point, or is there a secondary approach that would reduce unnecessary engagement with the outer battalions? I ask purely for efficiency.*

*Also — are the caterers doing anything specific, or is this metaphorical?*

*Regards,*
*Ren (the Summoned Hero)*

---

The exchange that followed over the next ten days produced six letters and several insights:

The southwestern gate was, confirmed, the structural weak point. Mordavel had been meaning to have it repaired for two years but the contractor kept cancelling.

The caterers were entirely literal. It was a tradition Mordavel had inherited from the Third Demon King, who had believed apocalypses should be marked with ceremony. Mordavel had never personally endorsed the tradition but felt it would be rude to cancel at this point.

And in the fourth letter, almost as an aside, Mordavel wrote: *I should be transparent: the Final Darkness has itself been postponed twice on my end, for reasons I will describe only as personal. I mention this not to excuse delay on either side, but in the spirit of the candid correspondence we appear to be conducting.*

Ren read that line three times and showed it to no one.
