# Chapter 4: A Very Noble Scouting Mission

Ren agreed to scout the Demon King's fortress rather than fight it immediately — which, Elda pointed out, was at minimum a step in the right direction.

The agreement was reached on a Thursday. Ren left Friday morning, provisions packed, sword belted correctly after only two attempts, and set off down the northern road toward the Obsidian Citadel with the purposeful expression of someone who had every intention of returning with useful information.

The report Ren returned with, four days later, covered five pages:

- The Red Lantern Inn (two nights, exceptional lamb stew, the innkeeper had a dog named Pudding who had learned to open latched doors)
- A wildflower field north of the Ashmarsh Valley ("purple and white, very scenic, worth preserving post-apocalypse")
- A broken bridge over the Sal River ("hazard for mounted forces, should be repaired eventually")
- A roadside shrine to an old harvest deity, currently unattended ("left a coin, seemed polite")
- One line at the bottom of the final page: *Obsidian Citadel observed from significant distance. Large. Dark. Seems like a lot.*

Elda read it twice.

"You didn't go inside."

"Scouting doesn't mean going inside. Scouting means observing. I observed."

"From how far away?"

"Far enough to have accurate impressions."

Petra leaned over to read the entry about Pudding and decided not to comment.

"The Citadel has outer walls forty feet high," Elda said, keeping her voice level with visible effort. "Guard rotations every three hours. A central spire that coordinates the shadow armies. I needed to know if there are weak points in the southwestern gate."

Ren looked genuinely apologetic.

"I didn't realize it needed to be *that specific*. I thought you wanted a general overview."

"I said 'strategic reconnaissance.'"

"I strategically reconnoitered the general region."

Elda took off her glasses, cleaned them, and put them back on. The view did not improve.

"Ren. The whole of the Sal Valley burned while you were eating lamb stew."

Ren looked out the window. There was more smoke on the horizon than last week. Definitively more. It had gone from a suggestion to a statement.

"All right," Ren said, sitting up straighter. "Real scouting. Specific. I can do that. Give me until Thursday."

"It's Wednesday."

"Then Thursday gives me adequate preparation time."

Elda looked at Petra. Petra looked at her scroll.

"Noted," Petra said.

---

Ren spent Thursday preparing for the real scouting mission, which involved reading both volumes of *Tactical Observation Methods* from the Archive library until very late, falling asleep at the desk, and dreaming productively about Pudding the door-opening dog.

The dream did not appear in the mission notes, but it did improve Ren's mood considerably.

Friday arrived.

Ren looked at it for a while from the window.

"Actually," Ren said to no one, "I want to be fresh. Saturday."
