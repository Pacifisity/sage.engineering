# Chapter 5: The First Attempt (Sort Of)

Ren left on a Saturday.

This was already remarkable. The horse was saddled by the time the sun was properly up, the sword was belted on correctly, and a light pack held three days of provisions, a water skin, and the annotated map folded wrong but technically present. Elda stood at the gate of the tower and said nothing, which was its own kind of ceremony.

The road north was beautiful in the wrongest way possible for an apocalypse's slow approach: tall pines, cool morning air, birdsong. Ren traveled two days without incident and camped sensibly by a stream on the second night.

On the third morning, standing on the Ashmarsh ridge, Ren saw the Obsidian Citadel for the first time from close range.

It was not large. It was *final*. Black stone stacked with deliberate permanence, the kind of architecture that says *I am not a building, I am an outcome*. The spire at its center leaked smoke that moved against the wind. The walls shimmered with something that wasn't heat.

Ren understood, standing there, what the word *dread* actually felt like in a body rather than in a plan.

The outer gates opened while Ren watched. From them came a battalion of shadow cavalry — horses made of dark smoke, lances tipped with something that didn't reflect light. They moved down the road away from the Citadel, heading south.

Not toward Ren.

But one rider at the rear turned, visor pointed up the ridge. Still. Certain.

Ren and the rider looked at each other across a full mile of open air.

The rider began climbing the ridge road.

Ren waited approximately four seconds.

Then pressed the mark on the left palm, said *Retur*, chose a point two days back —

— and arrived on the northern road on a crisp Saturday morning, heart hammering, provisions full, sword at the hip.

Ren sat down on a roadside stone for a long time.

A bird landed nearby, looked at Ren, and flew away.

Eventually Ren turned the horse south. Rode for two days. Arrived back at the tower at dusk, dismounted, handed the reins to the stable boy with an expression of someone who had conducted themselves professionally throughout.

Elda was waiting in the courtyard.

"You're back early," she said.

"Conditions were unfavorable," Ren said. "Wind. And also the general geopolitical situation."

"What does the geopolitical situation have to do with—"

"There were cavalry," Ren said, and walked inside.

Elda stood in the courtyard for a moment. She knew what *Retur* felt like in the atmosphere — a faint ripple, the air tasting briefly of turned earth and winter. She had felt it three days ago, from the north.

She had not mentioned this yet.

She went inside and made tea and also did not mention it while Ren ate dinner and told a detailed story about the ridge road wildflowers that had apparently reappeared on the second scouting mission too.

"They really are remarkable," Ren said.

"Yes," said Elda. "They really are."
