# Chapter 1: The Summoning

The three sages of Dawnreach had prepared everything perfectly.

The summoning circle glowed with twelve hours of careful chalk work. Sacred incense spiraled from seven bronze braziers. The ritual chant had been rehearsed until every syllable landed on the exact breath it deserved.

What they summoned was Ren.

Ren arrived face-down on the marble floor, arms spread like someone who had fallen asleep mid-gesture, still wearing house slippers and holding a half-eaten piece of toast.

Lead Sage Elda stared for a long moment.

"That's... the Chosen One?" asked Sage Petra.

"The stars were very specific," Elda replied carefully.

Ren rolled over and blinked at the vaulted stone ceiling, at the seven braziers, at the three robed figures. Ren sat up with the toast still in hand and looked around with the expression of someone cataloguing a mildly interesting dream.

"Oh," Ren said. "Where's this?"

"The kingdom of Dawnreach," Elda said, stepping forward. "We have summoned you because our world is dying. The Demon King Mordavel has raised an army of shadow and ash, and his forces encircle the last free cities. The old prophecy speaks of a hero—"

"Is that a real chandelier or carved stone?" Ren pointed upward.

"...Carved stone. The hero must—"

"That must have taken forever."

Elda breathed. She had spent forty years in preparation for this moment.

"The hero," she continued, "arrives with a power unlike any in this world. You have the ability to turn back time. To undo any moment, any wound, any battle, and begin it again."

Ren thought about this seriously for the first time.

"Is that right." Not a question. More of a slow inventory.

"We have seen it. The mark on your left palm—"

Ren looked at the left palm. There was indeed a mark, like a closed eye, the pupil shaped like an hourglass. Ren had always assumed it was a birthmark.

"It activates on intention," Elda said. "In moments of need, you need only press it and speak the word *Retur*, and time will fold back to the point you designate."

"And then I just... live the time again?"

"With full memory of what happened. Knowledge of every outcome."

Ren stood up, brushed toast crumbs off a rumpled shirt, and nodded like someone receiving directions to a moderately inconvenient location.

"Okay," Ren said. "And this Demon King — he's in a castle somewhere?"

"The Obsidian Citadel. Three days' ride north."

"Three days. Right." Ren looked toward the tall arched windows. It was late afternoon, gold and calm. "It's nearly evening. I'd probably need to sleep, get the lay of the land, find a weapon. I'm thinking maybe I start fresh in the morning?"

Elda opened her mouth.

"Actually," Ren said, noticing a fruit bowl on the dais. "Are those pears?"

"Yes, but—"

"Can I have one?"

Elda pinched the bridge of her nose. Beside her, Petra wrote *hero acquired — concerns noted* in the margin of her scroll.

That was one week ago. Ren was still in Dawnreach.
